The Joy of Learning Through Children’s Eyes

Empowering children through learning, even amidst the harshest challenges of war and conflict.

In the face of adversity, education remains the cornerstone of hope for children. The ongoing conflict between Israel and Lebanon has disrupted life across multiple regions, including areas like the Bekaa Valley and Bourj Hammoud, where Jusoor operates three vital education centers. This crisis has jeopardized not only the safety of children in host and refugee communities but also their fundamental right to learn.


As the academic year was set to begin on October 1st, preparations were in full swing to welcome students back to classrooms. However, the escalating security concerns forced a difficult decision: the suspension of in-person classes across all centers to protect students, teachers, and staff. But education cannot stop. To bridge this gap, Jusoor has swiftly transitioned to its award-winning remote learning initiative, “Azima.”


Developed during the COVID-19 pandemic, Azima leverages accessible platforms like WhatsApp to deliver lessons in math, English, Arabic, and life skills. This innovative approach ensures continuity in education during times of crisis. Recognized for its impact, Azima stands as a testament to adaptability and resilience in delivering education to vulnerable communities.


Jusoor’s Refugee Education team has been working tirelessly to adapt lessons and materials for this remote format. In addition to educational content, the program integrates psychosocial support sessions to provide students and families with a sense of stability. These sessions create safe spaces for guidance, coupled with mental health resources, including videos and booklets, designed to help families cope during these challenging times. For younger children, tailored activity booklets featuring tracing, coloring, and storytelling activities are helping nurture early learning skills.


This situation highlights the ongoing struggles of Syrian refugee children, who have already endured significant disruptions to their education due to the Syrian conflict. Now, the escalating violence in Lebanon threatens to rob them once again of their right to learn. Despite these obstacles, Jusoor remains steadfast in its commitment to ensuring these children have access to education.


In addition to educational efforts, Jusoor’s staff has stepped up to support the wider community by volunteering at a local kitchen that prepares daily meals for 700 families. This spirit of solidarity underscores the organization’s broader mission: to uplift and empower all those affected by the crisis, including the Lebanese host community.


Looking forward, Jusoor is exploring ways to extend the reach of its Azima program by sharing its expertise with other organizations. By doing so, it aims to ensure that education remains a beacon of hope, even in the most uncertain times. Through resilience, creativity, and collaboration, Jusoor continues to stand as a pillar of support for children and their families.


In the darkest moments, education is the light that guides us forward. Together, we can ensure that children’s futures are not defined by the challenges they face today.
